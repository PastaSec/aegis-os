# Power Outage Safety

Power outages can interrupt lighting, refrigeration, communications, medical devices, water systems, and heating or cooling. Plan for the outage before it starts.

## Immediate Actions

- Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed.
- Use flashlights instead of candles when possible.
- Disconnect sensitive electronics if surge risk is high.
- Check backup power for phones, radios, and medical equipment.
- Report outages through the local utility process when communications are available.

## Generator Safety

- Run generators outdoors only.
- Keep generators away from doors, windows, vents, garages, and enclosed spaces.
- Let equipment cool before refueling.
- Use outdoor-rated extension cords in good condition.
- Install and test carbon monoxide alarms.

## Extended Outage Notes

- Monitor heat, cold, and medical-device risk.
- Use community cooling, warming, or charging sites when available.
- Discard unsafe food when temperature history is uncertain.
- Keep fuel stored safely and legally.
