# Evacuation Planning

Evacuation is easier when routes, supplies, contacts, and destinations are decided before pressure is high.

## Prepare

- Learn local evacuation zones.
- Identify multiple routes away from the hazard area.
- Choose family or team meeting locations.
- Keep vehicle fuel or charging capacity available when possible.
- Prepare a go kit with documents, medicine, water, food, and chargers.
- Include pets, service animals, mobility needs, and medical equipment in the plan.

## When Ordered To Leave

- Leave early enough to avoid severe weather, flooding, or blocked roads.
- Follow official routes and local instructions.
- Carry identification, essential documents, cash, and medications.
- Tell a contact where you are going.
- Secure the home if time and conditions allow.

## If Shelter Is Needed

- Bring essential supplies, documents, medicine, and chargers.
- Follow shelter rules and staff instructions.
- Keep important items close and labeled.
- Preserve battery power and monitor official updates.
